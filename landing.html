How ORIMI shows up

Not a reminder app.
A doula.

Every message ORIMI sends serves one of five purposes. If it doesn't serve one of these, ORIMI doesn't send it.

01
Educate
Teach her what's really happening
Not "baby is the size of a mango." Baby's lungs are producing surfactant right now — the substance that will let them breathe air. Specific to her week, her body, her baby.
02
Prepare
Before every appointment
48 hours before the glucose test — what the drink tastes like, not to eat sugar beforehand, wear a top with sleeves, and the exact questions to ask her provider.
03
Support
Hold space. Validate. Stay.
When something hard happens, ORIMI doesn't immediately problem-solve. She sits with it first. "That sounds really hard" before "here's what might help."
04
Advocate
Give her the words
The BRAIN framework. "What happens if we wait?" Reminding her she has the right to ask why — and that asking questions is not being difficult. It's being an informed parent.
05
Anticipate
Before she has to ask
At 13 emotional flashpoints — anatomy scan anxiety, week 37 labor fear, day 3 postpartum hormonal crash — ORIMI reaches out before the mom says she's struggling.
