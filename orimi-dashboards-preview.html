This week — week 32

Baby's skull is staying soft on purpose

The plates will overlap during delivery — that's why newborns sometimes have cone-shaped heads. It resolves in days. This is perfect design, not a problem. Baby is also practicing breathing movements right now — rehearsing the motion so the body is ready the moment of birth.

Heartburn, back pain, trouble sleeping

Progesterone relaxes the valve between your stomach and esophagus — that's the heartburn. Smaller meals, don't lie down right after eating. Ready and not ready at the same time. Fear of labor is normal. So is excitement. All of it is allowed.

Nourishment this week

Protein 80-100g daily — baby is gaining half a pound a week

6 Medjool dates daily — start now

Clinical research shows shorter first stage labor and less need for augmentation. The prostaglandins in dates help ripen the cervix.

Wild salmon or walnuts

DHA for baby's brain — it's building billions of neural connections right now.

Magnesium for cramps and sleep

Pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate, epsom salt bath. Check magnesium glycinate with midwife.

Herb of the week

Red raspberry leaf tea

2-3 cups per day — work up gradually

Tones the uterine muscle — may support more efficient contractions during labor. Use loose-leaf, steep 10+ minutes. Check with midwife first especially if you have history of preterm labor.

Holistic support

Webster-certified chiropractor — weekly now

Baby is settling into position. Pelvic alignment gives baby the best chance to go head-down. Not about cracking the back — about making space.

Breathing practice — daily

Wave breathing — 4 in, 6 out

Breathe in through the nose for 4 counts. Out through the mouth for 6 counts. The longer exhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system — it literally tells the body "we are safe." Practice now so it's automatic during labor.

How she's feeling this week

Mostly good — but carrying a lot right now

Her back pain is real. Her center of gravity has completely shifted and her body is adjusting to carrying extra weight at the front. She's also dealing with heartburn, trouble sleeping, and moments of fear about labor mixed with excitement.

When she says she's tired or uncomfortable, she's not being dramatic. Her cardiovascular system is working harder, her blood volume has increased by 50%, and her body is preparing for the biggest physical event of her life.

One thing you can do today

Warm rice sock on her lower back tonight

Fill a tube sock with uncooked rice and microwave it for 90 seconds. Apply to her lower back before bed. This isn't just a nice gesture — it's actually helping with the muscle tension from her shifted center of gravity.

Offer to rub her feet too. Foot massage improves circulation and genuinely reduces the edema (swelling) she's experiencing. 10 minutes is enough to make a real difference.

Baby this week

Baby is practicing breathing right now

Not breathing air yet — rehearsing the motion so the body is ready the moment of birth. Baby's skull is also staying soft and flexible so it can move through the birth canal.

Talk to her belly. Baby can hear your voice. The sounds heard most in the womb become familiar and comforting after birth — your voice will be one of them.

Your role

She'd love you there if you can make it. Your job is to be present — not to ask all the questions, not to manage the information. Let her lead. Listen. Ask if there's anything she wants you to remember.

How to support her right now

Learn the counter-pressure technique

During labor, firm pressure on her lower back with the heel of your hand can transform her experience of back labor. Practice now so it's muscle memory when the time comes.

Create her oxytocin nest

Oxytocin — the hormone that drives labor — flows when she feels safe, private, and unobserved. Dim lights, familiar music, warmth, only people who make her feel safe.

Should be packed and in the car by now. Going through it together is practical AND reassuring for both of you.
