What is happening — week 32

Baby's skull is staying soft on purpose

The plates will overlap during delivery — that's why newborns sometimes have cone-shaped heads. It resolves in days. This is perfect design, not a problem.

Practicing breathing movements right now

Not breathing air yet — rehearsing the motion. Lungs are producing surfactant, the substance that will let baby breathe outside the womb. Every day inside matters.

Heartburn, back pain, trouble sleeping

Progesterone relaxes the valve between stomach and esophagus — that's the heartburn. Smaller meals help. For sleep: pillow between the knees, slightly propped up. This is not failing — this is third trimester.

Ready and not ready at the same time

Fear of labor is normal. So is excitement. So is feeling done with being pregnant. All of it is allowed. All of it means you're paying attention.

Nourishment this week

Protein 80-100g daily

Baby is gaining half a pound a week right now — mostly muscle and fat. The body needs building blocks. Eggs, Greek yogurt, lentils, chicken, fish.

6 Medjool dates daily — start now

Clinical research: women who ate 6 dates daily in the last 4-6 weeks had shorter first stage labor, higher cervical dilation on admission, and less need for augmentation. It's the prostaglandins.

DHA — baby's brain is building now

Billions of neural connections forming this week. Wild salmon 2x this week or walnuts daily. Plant-based? Algae-based DHA covers it.

Magnesium for leg cramps and sleep

Pumpkin seeds, dark chocolate, epsom salt bath at night. Or magnesium glycinate supplement — check with midwife first.

Herb of the week

Red raspberry leaf tea

2-3 cups per day — work up gradually

Tones the uterine muscle and may support more efficient contractions during labor. Use loose-leaf, steep for at least 10 minutes for full benefit. Not extract capsules. Check with midwife first, especially with any history of preterm labor.

Holistic support this week

Webster-certified chiropractor — weekly now

Baby is settling into position. Pelvic alignment gives baby the best chance to go head-down. This isn't about cracking the back — it's about making space. Start now, not at week 36.

Pre-birth acupuncture

Weekly sessions from 36 weeks prepare the cervix, support baby's position, and reduce labor anxiety. If baby is breech, moxibustion has clinical evidence for turning. Not fringe — integrated into midwifery care for centuries.

What this appointment is

Standard third trimester check. They'll measure the belly (fundal height), listen to baby's heartbeat via doppler, check blood pressure, and start checking baby's position using Leopold's maneuvers — pressing on the belly to feel where the head is. Quick visit, usually 15-20 minutes. It doesn't hurt.

How to prepare

Wear a two-piece outfit — they'll need to measure the belly

Drink water before — full bladder helps the doppler pick up heartbeat

Bring a list of any symptoms, questions, or concerns — don't rely on memory

Questions to ask — use the BRAIN framework

"Is baby head-down yet? If not, what are my options and how much time do I have?"
"When do appointments move to weekly? What changes at 36 weeks?"
"What symptoms should make me call immediately between now and my next visit?"

Breathing practice — daily

Wave breathing

Breathe in through the nose for 4 counts. Imagine riding up a wave. Exhale slow and steady through the mouth for 6 counts. Ride down. The contraction will end. They always end. The exhale being longer than the inhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system — it literally tells the body: we are safe. Practice 5 minutes daily so it's automatic during labor, not something to think about.

How she's feeling this week

Mostly good — but carrying a lot right now

Her back pain is real. Her center of gravity has completely shifted and her body is adjusting to carrying extra weight at the front. She's also dealing with heartburn, trouble sleeping, and moments of fear about labor mixed with excitement. All of it is normal. All of it is a lot.

When she says she's tired or uncomfortable, she's not being dramatic. Her blood volume has increased by 50% and her body is preparing for the biggest physical event of her life. Both things can be true at once.

Prepare

One thing you can do today

Warm rice sock on her lower back tonight

Fill a tube sock with uncooked rice and microwave it for 90 seconds. Apply to her lower back before bed. This isn't just a nice gesture — it's actually helping with the muscle tension from her shifted center of gravity.

Also: offer to rub her feet. Foot massage improves circulation and genuinely reduces the edema (swelling) she's experiencing in her ankles and feet. 10 minutes is enough to make a real difference. This is clinical, not romantic — though it can be both.

Baby this week

Baby is practicing breathing right now

Not breathing air yet — rehearsing the motion so the body is ready the moment of birth. Baby's skull is also staying soft and flexible so it can move through the birth canal. That cone shape some newborns have? It resolves in days. Perfectly designed.

Talk to her belly. Baby can hear your voice now. The sounds heard most in the womb become familiar and comforting after birth — your voice will be one of the first things this baby recognizes.

What happens

Provider checks baby's position, measures her belly, listens to the heartbeat via doppler, checks blood pressure. Quick visit — about 15-20 minutes.

Your role

She'd love you there if you can make it. Your job is to be present — not to ask all the questions, not to manage the information, just to be there. Let her lead. Listen. Ask her afterward if there's anything she wants you to remember.

Support

How to support her right now

Learn the counter-pressure technique

During labor, firm pressure on her lower back with the heel of your hand or a tennis ball can transform her experience of back labor. This is one of the most powerful things a partner can do. Practice now — it needs to be muscle memory, not something to figure out mid-contraction.

Create her oxytocin nest

Oxytocin is the hormone that drives labor. It flows when she feels safe, private, and unobserved. Adrenaline (from fear, bright lights, strangers, feeling rushed) directly suppresses it. Start thinking now: dim lights, her playlist, warmth, only people who make her feel completely safe. This is not about aesthetics. It's about biology.

Check the hospital bag together

It should be packed and in the car by now. Going through it together is practical AND reassuring for both of you. Make sure the car seat is installed. Know which entrance to use at the birth location. Drive the route once.

This week ahead

Week 32 is when birth stops being theoretical and starts being real — for both of you. That's appropriate. Fear and excitement at the same time is the right response to something this significant. Let her name it. Ask her what she's feeling. Don't try to fix the fear — just be with her in it.

"The most powerful thing you can do right now is be steady. She doesn't need you to fix anything. She needs to know you're not going anywhere. Your calm is contagious. Your presence matters more than you know."
